About Hatua Zetu Foundation (HZF)

Hatua Zetu Foundation (HZF) is a feminist-led organization founded in 2020 amid the Covid-19 pandemic. Our mission is to support communities by demystifying myths around Covid, raising awareness about ending violence against women and girls, and focusing on the well-being of children with autism. We were officially registered in 2023.

Our Vision

HZF envisions an equitable, just, and safe society where young women, girls, and young people with autism can assert their rights and defend their autonomy, lives, and livelihoods.

Our Mission

Our mission is to promote social justice and the rights of young women and girls in all diversities, as well as young people with autism, through art, creativity, advocacy, and collective power.

Our Goals

We aim to create a safe and inclusive space for girls, young women, and young people with autism to express their thoughts and opinions on their well-being and the matters that affect them the most, and to be part of the change.

Our Approach

We strive to build sustainable and equitable communities by challenging oppressive structures through the power of art, advocacy, and innovation, while centering human rights and dignity. We work strategically in partnership with young feminists and activists within our networks to provide the necessary strategies for uplifting daily struggles, widening access, and advancing the rights of women, girls, non-binary people, young people with autism, and other marginalized groups.
